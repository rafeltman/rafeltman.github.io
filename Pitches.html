Calories Per Capita


In 2010, each person in the United States had nearly 4,000 calories available to them in a day. In Afghanistan, only 2,200. I'd like to create some visualization of what these dicrepencies in total food availability look like: Not a map, if I can help it, but some other manner of infographic I haven't learned how to make yet. This would be reminiscent of this photo essay, and incorporate more data and conclusions about food scarcity in various countries. I would, of course, address the fact that high calorie availability per capita doesn't necessarily equal a lack of hunger.
